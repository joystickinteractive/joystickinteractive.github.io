Testing

Testing

Nothing goes out without some testing. There are few absolutes in these guidelines, but this is one.

Testing Environments

To say it is difficult to test a site that is constantly changing is an understatement. Our server setup typically has the following three environments:

  • dev
  • stage
  • production

A release moves from dev to stage and then once tested there, into the production environment. Do not make fiddly little changes when QA is in the middle of testing your work.

Supported Browsers & Devices

This is often times dictated by whatâ€™s in the Statement of Work (SOW), but when it is not you should target the following devices and browsers.

  • IE 10 / 11
  • Firefox (latest) (Mac & Windows)
  • Chrome (latest) (Mac & Windows)
  • Safari (latest) (Mac)
  • iOS (latest) (iPhone 5 iOS 7 & iPhone 6 iOS 8)
  • Android (latest) (Kit Kat (4.4.4) or Lollipop(5.0))

Bug Tracking

Our preferred means of tracking issues is Done Done. Sometimes clients ask us to use their existing systems though, and there are some who would like to move to Jira for tracking bugs. We are also very friendly to using Github Issues for tracking bugs in our work.
